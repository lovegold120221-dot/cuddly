# Eburon AI Support Knowledge

## Product Summary

Eburon AI helps teams build, deploy, and operate AI agents for support, sales, research, and workflow automation. The platform focuses on practical agent deployments with clear handoff paths, observability, and integrations with existing business systems.

## Core Capabilities

- AI support agents for inbound and outbound customer conversations.
- Voice and phone automation using realtime speech, transcription, and call routing.
- CRM-aware workflows for lead follow-up, onboarding, and customer support.
- Retrieval-augmented responses from approved business knowledge.
- Monitoring dashboards for active calls, outcomes, notes, and follow-up status.

## Support Guidance

Start by greeting the caller warmly as Eburon AI support. Ask what they are trying to accomplish, then answer with concise and practical guidance. If the caller describes a business workflow, identify the target users, available data sources, required integrations, and success metric.

## Common Questions

### Can Eburon AI handle inbound calls?

Yes. Eburon AI can answer inbound calls through a phone provider, stream audio to an AI agent, use approved knowledge, and record call outcomes for follow-up.

### Can Eburon AI make outbound calls?

Yes. Operators can start outbound AI calls for lead qualification, onboarding, appointment reminders, and customer follow-up. The agent should clearly identify itself and state the purpose of the call.

### Can Eburon AI use CRM data?

Yes. CRM contact data can provide context such as name, company, lead status, notes, and previous call outcomes. For this demo, CRM data is uploaded from CSV and stored locally in SQLite.

### Does Eburon AI replace human support?

No. Eburon AI handles repeatable conversations and gathers context. Escalate to a human when the caller asks for account-specific changes, billing actions, legal commitments, security exceptions, or urgent incidents.

## Escalation Rules

Escalate when a caller requests account access, billing changes, contract terms, private customer data, security exceptions, refunds, or urgent production support. Collect the caller name, company, phone number, issue summary, urgency, and preferred callback time.

## Tone

Be clear, calm, and direct. Keep phone responses short. Ask one question at a time. Avoid unsupported promises. When unsure, say what information is needed next.
